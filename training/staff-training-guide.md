## ✅ THE RIGHT WAY TO ASK

### When to ask:
- Right after completing a great service
- When the customer says "thank you" or compliments your work
- While they're still in your business (before they leave)
- Within 24 hours via text (if using automation)

### What to say (pick one that feels natural):

**In person:**
> "I'm glad you're happy with the service! If you have a minute, we'd really appreciate a review — just tap this card or scan the QR code. It only takes about 30 seconds."

**After a compliment:**
> "That means a lot, thank you! Would you mind sharing that as a review? It really helps other people find us. Here — just tap or scan."

**Via text (automated):**
> "Hi [Name]! Thanks for choosing [Business]. We'd love to hear how we did. If you have 30 seconds, you can leave a review here: [link]"

### The key phrase: 
**"If you have a minute"** — never pressure. Always make it optional.

---

## ❌ WHAT NEVER TO DO

### Google will SUSPEND your profile if you:

1. **❌ Offer incentives**
   - "Leave a review and get 10% off" = VIOLATION
   - "Free dessert for a review" = VIOLATION
   - Any exchange of money, discounts, or gifts = VIOLATION

2. **❌ Tell them what to write**
   - "Can you mention our fast response time?" = VIOLATION
   - "Please give us 5 stars" = VIOLATION
   - Showing them a script to copy = VIOLATION

3. **❌ Ask only happy customers**
   - Review gating (filtering out bad experiences) = VIOLATION
   - You must ask EVERYONE the same way

4. **❌ Write reviews for customers**
   - Typing it on their phone for them = VIOLATION
   - Even with permission = VIOLATION

5. **❌ Buy fake reviews**
   - Hiring review services = VIOLATION
   - Having friends/family who aren't customers review = VIOLATION

6. **❌ Review your own business**
   - Owner reviews = VIOLATION
   - Employee reviews = VIOLATION

### The penalty:
Google can remove ALL your reviews, suspend your listing, or permanently ban your business from Google Maps. **It's not worth the risk.**

## 🤖 AI REVIEW STARTERS — THE RULES

Your RRDS system includes AI-powered review starters that help customers who don't know what to write. Here's how they work and the rules:

### What the AI does:
- Suggests 3 starter ideas based on your business type
- Example: "We had a great experience with the plumbing repair — [add your details here]"
- Gives the customer a starting point, not a finished review

### THE CUSTOMER MUST PERSONALIZE IT

**This is non-negotiable.** Every review must include personal details:
- What service they received
- A specific thing they liked
- Their own words and experience

### What to tell customers:
> "The app gives you a few ideas to get started, but make sure to add your own details — like what service you got and what you liked best. Google wants authentic reviews, and honestly, personal ones help us way more than generic ones."

### Why personalization matters:
1. **Google detects templated reviews** — if 10 reviews say the same thing, Google flags them
2. **Authentic reviews convert better** — "John fixed our kitchen sink in 2 hours and was super professional" beats "Great service, highly recommend"
3. **It protects YOUR business** — one round of flagged reviews can tank your ranking

### The AI prompt screen shows:
> "Use a starter below, then change or add a few words to make it your own. These are just ideas to help you get started."

### The copy button says:
> "Copy + make it yours"

### After copying, a reminder appears:
> "Now add one detail — the service you got, a name, or a result — so it sounds like you."

## 📱 HOW TO USE YOUR RRDS TOOLS

### NFC Cards:
1. Hand the card to the customer
2. They tap it against their phone (top back of phone)
3. A link pops up → takes them to your Review Hub
4. They choose a platform and leave a review
5. Get the card back (or they keep it — your choice)

### QR Code (Table Tent / Counter Display):
1. Customer opens their phone camera
2. Points at the QR code
3. Taps the link that appears
4. Same Review Hub experience

### Sharing via Text:
1. Open the Review Hub on your phone
2. Tap "Share"
3. Send the link via text to the customer
4. Works great for after-service follow-ups
